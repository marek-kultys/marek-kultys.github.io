Case study

Winning stakeholder support for Vivli

{% include divider.html %}

My role: Design Lead / contributor

Project type: Client project

Year: 2016

Contribution: Design leadership, Client leadership, UX design, Vision prototyping, Qualitative research

Outcome: Vivli successfully secured backing from consortium partners

The Challenge

Vivli is a global non-profit on a mission to maximise the use and access to clinical trial data for the benefit of the wider biomedical R&D community.

In 2016, Vivli approached with technical specs of their new data sharing platform. They needed help with two key challenges holding up their progress:

  • Validate Vivli’s vision with target users
  • Get Vivli’s consortium of partners behind this vision

I led concept modelling for the Vivli data sharing platform based on Vivli’s technical specs.

I designed high-level user flows based on Vivli’s technical specs to inform prototyping.

My Approach

To address both challenged, I worked with Vivli founders to translate their technical specification into a tangible design proposal delivered as a clickable prototype. I then used the prototype to validate the product and communicate its vision and value to stakeholders.


Clickable vision prototype showing the study search experience, part of the Data Requester flow.


Video demo showing the user flow of the Data Submitter experience, based on the vision prototype.

The Result

I validated Vivli's vision in research with experts. I used the prototype as design stimulus in conversation to help elicit feedback and action it in subsequent iterations.

With the prototype, I produce a video demo showing Vivli’s vision in tangible, realistic terms. It helped Vivli founders impress their partners and win their support.
