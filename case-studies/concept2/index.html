Case study

Preparing patients for a clinical trial with Concept2 training

My role: Design Lead / contributor

Project type: Client project, B2B

Year: 2017

Contribution: Content production and localisation, Instructional design, Prototyping, Project management

Outcome: Clinical trial successfully onboarded and trained patients

The Challenge

It has been reported that only 40%â€“60% of COPD patients adhere to prescribed drug regimen, and only 1 out of 10 perform inhalation correctly (source). Pharma companies aim to improve health outcomes by building digital tools to help with adherence and technique.

In 2017, Novartis needed a design for a new patient-facing app that paired with a smart inhaler, tracked medication adherence, sent reminders, and coached COPD patients. A phase III study was to evaluate rhe benefits of this digital intervention. For onboarding, a new training experience was needed to train study participants in pairing of the inhaler and taking the inhalations correctly.

I invited Vivli's technical leadership to co-design.

The Solution

As part of a broader app development project, I led the design and production of an in-app video onboarding and training experience that prepared patients to use their digital inhaler in a clinical trial.

I invited Vivli's technical leadership to co-design.

My Approach

In the design stage, I storyboarded the video, prototyped and iterated inhalation sequences (tested on myself and team as patient proxies) and secured regulatory sign-offs.

In production, I hired actors and directed filming, voiceover and foley recordings, editing, and post-production. I also managed translation and localisation into 7 languages.

I invited Vivli's technical leadership to co-design.

The Result

The video was used in the Novartis NCT03379233 clinical trial that aimed to evaluate the effects of digital interventions on patient adherence and engagement.

Patients were successfully onboarded and trained, but the trial was discontinued due to inhaler device malfunction.
