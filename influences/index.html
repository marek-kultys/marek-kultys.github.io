I've been inspired by these people and their work:

Edward Tufte

Reading Tufte's The Visual Display of Quantitative Information shaped the way I approach and think about visual information. Although strongly rooted in the print medium, most of Tufte's observations still apply to the digital context.

Radical Candor

I revisit Kim Scott's book regularly to re-align my compass on things that are important to me in being a UX manager. Kim's advice to care personally and challenge directly (illustrated by vivid anegdotes from her own career in tech companies) has shaped my approach to leadership and management.

Isotype

Isotype's visual style, ingenious charts, and clever narratives have always been an inspiration for me in my work with data. But only after reading The Transformer, I also appreciated the value of the process that Otto and Marie Neurath established to distill something complex into something playful.

Only An Ocean Between by L. Secor Florence with 18 charts designed by the Isotype Institute

James King and Science Practice

I've been lucky to have James as a friend and a mentor since 2011 when I started working with him at Science Practice. For almost 8 years I've been able to grow beside him as a designer and a leader, learning how to apply a design mindset to many challenges and domains.

Medium

For me — when it comes to the discourse on product design, UX, and design leadership — the best thing about Medium is the diversity of opinion presented there. My favourite authors include: Intercom, Nikki Anderson, Jared M Spool, Matt Ström, Matt Griffin, Cassie Kozyrkov, Buster Benson, Thomas P Seager, and the Chan Zuckerberg Science Initiative.

Jake Knapp and John Zeratsky

I wish I had Jake's Design Sprint book when I was a student! Today, whenever I visit my home country to teach a design class at an arts college, I donate a copy of the book to their library so that new students can have access to it early on.

Currently, I'm reading Jake's second book: Make Time.

Adrian Frutiger

I read Der Mensch und Seine Zeichen in Polish (sadly, no English translation yet). Frutiger's analysis of the visual language (including typography, iconography, and layout) inspires most of my design work to this day.

Scott McCloud

I recommend reading Understanding Comics to anyone interested in visual communication. Two things that made a lasting impact on me are McCloud's explanations of image transitions and the "reality—meaning—abstraction" pyramid.

R. D. Sinelnikov

I grew up in a family of medical doctors, surrounded by medial handbooks from the earliest years of my life. There was one book in particular that I loved browsing and that, for me, for the first time brought together the concepts of the life sciences and arts: Sinelnikov's Atlas of Human Anatomy.
