During my design career I transitioned across many design disciplines (industrial design, data visualisation, user experience, digital product design) and also many subject domains (life sciences, healthcare, AI). I learnt that the single most important skill for a cross-disciplinary designer is the ability to learn quickly and on the go.

Edward Tufte

Reading Tufte's The Visual Display of Quantitative Information shaped the way I think about and approach visual information. Although strongly rooted in the print medium, most of Tufte's observations still apply to the digital context.

Design Sprint

I wish I had this book when I was at college! Now, whenever I visit my home country (Poland) to teach a design classe at an arts college, I donate a copy of Design Sprint to their library so that new designers can read it early on.

James King and Science Practice

I've been lucky to have James as a friend and a mentor since 2011 when I started working with him at Science Practice. For almost 8 years I've been able to grow beside him as a designer and a leader, learning how to apply a design mindset to so many diverse challenges and domains.
